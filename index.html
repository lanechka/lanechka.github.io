News for April 2022

The Pattern of the Month for April is (somewhat counterintuitively) the Shawl for the 17th of May. It was originally designed to be worn on Norway's Constitution Day -- 17 May. The pattern is 20% off until the end of the month; no coupon is necessary as the discount is automatically applied at checkout. If you start knitting now, maybe you'll be done in time for the 17th next month!

Easter will soon be here, and in Norway that means it's time for påskekrim -- Easter crime. This tradition dates back to the 1920s, and nowadays involves reading crime novels and watching crime series on TV at Easter. With this in mind, I've reviewed the Norwegian graphic novel Magnus Carlsen og Sjakkmorderen (Magnus Carlsen and the Chess Killer). In this infinitely silly and action-filled story, a serial killer has divided Oslo into a chess board and presents world champion Magnus Carlsen with chess riddles that he must solve in order to determine the location of the next murder. Co-author Aleksander Kirkwood Brown shared this review on his Facebook page, calling it "interesting and reflective."

Lastly, my newest pattern, the Audur shawl, is now out on Ravelry. Named for the wife of Gísli Súrsson from the Saga of Gisli the Outlaw, it features a stockinette stitch body and an Estonian lace border.
