Along the Whale Road

This collection highlights yarns from Norway, Denmark, Gotland, Öland, the Isle of Man, Iceland, Shetland, the Orkneys, the Faroes, and the Hebrides -- all places where Norsemen visited, settled, and sometimes conquered.


Svanlaug

Svanlaug is a semi-circular shawl with both Estonian and Shetland motifs. Patterning is worked on both RS and WS; there are no rest rows. While the sample shawl was knitted in fingering weight yarn, the shawl can also be worked in laceweight. Instructions for enlarging the shawl by working more repeats of the final mesh motif are given in the pattern.

Gunnhild

The Gunnhild scarf features a simple lace pattern that is delineated by twisted-stitch cables. The scarf can be easily shortened, lengthened, and widened. Instructions for doing so are given in the pattern. While the sample was worked in a DK yarn, it would also look great in worsted.

Vivja

Vivja is a scarf with lace and nupps. It’s knitted in a single piece from the bottom up. The pattern assumes that you can read charts and have some experience knitting lace. A link is provided for 2 techniques to work nupps.

Vitastjerna

Vitastjerna's graceful lace and undulating cables are an elegant addition to fall and winter wardrobes. The sample scarf was worked in fingering weight yarn; it would also look good in DK weight. Instructions to change the length of the scarf are given in the pattern.

Ingiridh

Ingiridh is a semi-circular shawl with Shetland lace motifs. The shawl is worked in stockinette stitch with patterning on every row (i.e. there are no rest rows). The shawl’s size can be adjusted by working more or fewer repeats of the final diamond chart. Instructions to do so are given in the pattern.

Jorunn

Jorunn features an inverted flower basket motif with nupp embellishments at the very end. The shawl’s main pattern is modular and therefore it can be easily resized. Instructions to do so are given in the pattern.

Gytha

Gytha's main motif is a variation on the traditional horseshoe from Shetland/fishtail from Estonia offset with garter stitch. It is knitted in two parts and grafted at the center. The scarf can be easily shortened, lengthened, and widened. Instructions for doing so are given in the pattern.
